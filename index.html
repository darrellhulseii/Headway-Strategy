Brand Foundation

Before you post a single reel or run a single ad, you need your "one line" — what people say about you when you're not in the room. Ryan Magin's entire content and brand philosophy starts here.

📎 Ryan Magin @ryanmagin — LinkedIn, Viral Edits founder, Chris Van Vliet podcast

"Your brand isn't about what you claim. It's about what sticks when you're not in the room. If your brand can't be distilled into one repeatable truth, it won't scale — no matter how good the marketing looks."

Ryan Magin — @ryanmagin
💡 Assignment #1 — Do This Before Anything Else: Write one sentence that completes: "I help [specific person] do [specific outcome] without [specific frustration]." This is your one line. Every piece of content, every ad, every Headway bio headline must pass through this filter. If it doesn't reinforce your one line, cut it or rewrite it.

Building Your One Line

Component 1 — Your Person
Who exactly

Be specific. "Couples" is not a person. "Newly married couples in their 30s who are shocked that this person they loved is now the person they fight with most" — that's a person. The narrower your person, the louder your signal reaches them. Magin: if you're reaching everybody, you're reaching nobody.

LMFT person examples: Newly married couples · First-responder families · New parents losing their partnership · Multicultural couples · High-conflict couples who've already tried therapy · Couples considering divorce
Component 2 — The Outcome
Transformation, not service

People don't want "couples therapy." They want to feel heard by their partner again. They want to stop having the same fight. They want to like each other again. Name the outcome they actually want — not the clinical service you provide.

LMFT outcome examples: Actually feel heard · Stop the loop argument · Reconnect after having kids · Trust each other again · Say the thing they've been avoiding · Stop feeling like roommates
Component 3 — Remove the Frustration
The objection inside the positioning

Build the main barrier removal into your positioning. "Without waiting months for a slot" or "without feeling like you're being analyzed." For Headway users: "without the insurance guessing game" is your most powerful removal — name it in your one line.

Frustration removals: Without feeling judged · Without waiting months · Without the insurance headache · Without having to explain your cultural background · Without rehashing everything from childhood

"If you're trying to reach everybody, you'll definitely reach nobody. Niche down so hard it feels uncomfortable — that's where the audience is."

Ryan Magin — personal brand positioning

L1

Too broad. You reach no one specifically. This is where most therapists stop when describing themselves.

L2

Credential, not positioning. No differentiation from 10,000 other LMFTs in a directory.

Couples Therapist in [City]

Adds geography. Better. Still generic. Your Headway profile probably already says this.

"You should see my therapist — she specifically works with couples who've done therapy before and it didn't work." That's the goal. When that happens, the content is doing its job.

The Expert-as-Influencer Shift

"The next major social media trend: experts as influencers. Not creators, not brand mascots — actual experts. Founders, marketers, practitioners. The people building the product should be the ones talking about it. Authority converts. It builds trust, community, and pipeline."

Ryan Magin — LinkedIn
What this means for you

You are the expert in the room. You have years of clinical training that the average person desperately needs access to. An LMFT posting about communication patterns, attachment styles, or how to stop a loop argument isn't "promoting a practice" — it's democratizing clinical knowledge. That is your content purpose. Clients come as a byproduct of the authority you build.

The business model behind it

When you educate on social, you build a trust bank before a client ever contacts you. By the time they book on Headway, they've watched 8 of your videos, they already agree with your approach, and they've told their partner about you. Your first session converts like a fifth — because the getting-to-know-you phase happened on Instagram. This compounds over time in a way paid ads alone never will.

Magin's Content System

Ryan Magin built Viral Edits from zero to $500K in year one on a small set of repeatable video principles. These apply directly to an LMFT building a practice-filling social presence. Every principle is translated with a therapist-specific application.

📎 Ryan Magin @ryanmagin — Chris Van Vliet podcast, LinkedIn, Viral Edits agency

The Hook Quadrant

"Pay close attention to the first 3 seconds of your video. Provide something thought-provoking that gets people to stop scrolling. Say what is being said in a way that relates to the audience. The more personal story you attach to the hook, the more people will watch the whole thing."

Ryan Magin — Chris Van Vliet podcast
The Magin Hook Rule for Therapists: Your first 3 seconds must do one thing — make the viewer feel like this video was made specifically for them. Not for "people in relationships." For THEM, in their exact situation, right now. The hook is not an announcement. It's a mirror held up to their face.
Hook Type A — The Mirror
Show them themselves

Describe their internal experience so accurately they feel seen before you've offered anything. No framing. No setup. Just the experience — stated plainly, with clinical precision that reads as human warmth.

Formula: "When you [deeply specific experience] and nobody around you understands why."
EX

"When you love someone but feel completely alone inside the relationship."

EX

"When you're trying to apologize but you can't stop defending yourself at the same time."

EX

"When you don't even know what you're fighting about anymore — it just keeps happening."

Hook Type B — The Provocation
Disagree with what they believe

Magin: "Controversy is always the best recipe." For therapists, this means stating a clinical truth that contradicts a cultural belief. The viewer stops scrolling because the statement doesn't match what they've been told.

Formula: "[Common advice] is actually making [your relationship / situation] worse."
EX

"The way you were taught to apologize is making your relationship worse."

EX

"Couples therapy doesn't save relationships. Here's what actually does."

EX

"Listening more is not the answer. Here's what actually helps."

Hook Type C — The Clinical Fact
Surprising information, plain language

Magin: "Matter of facts, practical people problems." A research-backed clinical insight delivered without jargon stops the scroll because it's new information that immediately applies to their life. Lead with the fact, not the framing.

Formula: "Most people don't know that [clinical insight in plain language]."
EX

"Most people don't know their insurance probably covers couples therapy."

EX

"There's a clinical name for the argument you keep having — and it's not about what you think it's about."

EX

"What Gottman's 40 years of research shows happy couples do after a fight — most couples do the opposite."

Hook Type D — The Personal Story
Human truth builds profile visits

Magin: "The more personal story you can attach to the hook, the more people will watch the whole thing." For therapists, this means professional observations delivered with human warmth — a pattern you see, a moment from a (anonymized) session, something that shifted your perspective.

Formula: "Something I see in almost every [couple / family / person] I work with — and most of them don't know it's happening."
EX

"A pattern I see in almost every couple I work with — and it's not about communication."

EX

"The moment I realized I was approaching couples therapy wrong — and what changed."

Watch Time Is the Only Metric That Matters

"TikTok is driven on watch time. The sole purpose should be to make the person watch the whole video. You have to make them watch 20 seconds. It sounds easy, but try to make them watch a full 20 seconds."

Ryan Magin — Chris Van Vliet podcast
The 3-second rule

The hook must stop scroll in 3 seconds. No intro. No "Hi everyone!" No "So today I wanted to share..." — those are skip triggers. Lead with the mirror, the provocation, or the fact. Open mid-sentence if you have to. Start at the interesting part.

Skip-death openers to never use: "Hi everyone!" / "Welcome back" / "So today I'm going to talk about..." / "I've been thinking a lot lately about..."
The secondary hook (8–12 sec)

Re-engage viewers who made it past the opener but are about to lose interest. A reframe, a surprising clinical fact, or a "and what this actually means is..." dropped at the 8–12 second mark pulls them back in for the payoff.

Secondary hook examples: "And the reason this keeps happening — and almost nobody talks about this — is..." or "Here's the part that surprises most people when I explain it in session..."
The payoff (end strong)

End with something they can do, feel, or share. Magin: "When they go to your profile, there has to be something else there." Your payoff must make them want the next video. End with a tool, a reframe, or a question that generates comments.

Strong endings: "Try this tonight and notice what changes." / "Most couples never realize this — now you do." / "What's the version of this in your relationship?" / "Save this for when you need it."

Magin's Rules — Do This, Never Do This

✅ What drives reach — do these
✓
Add captions to every single video

Most people watch without sound. Magin specifically names captions as a non-negotiable basic. If there are no captions, you're invisible to the majority of your audience.

✓
Repost content that works (without apology)

"I haven't made a fresh video in 2 months." Evergreen clinical insight doesn't expire. If a reel worked 4 months ago, repost it. New people see it every day. Consistency over novelty.

✓
Check analytics weekly, double down on winners

"Look at which videos are doing better, then make more of those but make them better." This is the entire optimization system — not a tool, not a trick. Just this, repeatedly.

✓
Cross-post to TikTok and Reels simultaneously

Same video, two platforms, double the reach. Magin built his agency on this principle. Your effort is in the content, not the platform management.

✓
Every video must stand completely alone

The algorithm will show your video to a cold stranger. No "as I mentioned last week" — no references to past videos. Each piece of content is someone's first encounter with you.

🚫 What kills reach — never do these
✗
Gendered openers that limit audience

"What's up guys?" — Magin specifically flags this. Gendered openers signal to the algorithm who the content is for, cutting your potential reach in half before anyone sees it.

✗
Hashtags to define your audience

"Hashtags box in the video before it starts." Use at most 3–5 very broad tags. Let the content itself signal audience to the algorithm, not the tags.

✗
Crisis vocabulary in content

Magin notes suppressed word categories. For therapy content: "suicide," "depression," "anxiety disorder," and crisis-adjacent terms limit distribution. Use clinical-adjacent plain language: "low mood," "overwhelm," "feeling stuck," "hard seasons."

✗
Trends and dances unrelated to your expertise

"In my world I have never done a dance or trend. If I'm turning this into a business, I can't rely on external factors." Build on evergreen clinical authority — not expiring audio trends.

✗
Leading with credentials, flexing, or credentials-as-brand

"TikTok doesn't do well with the flexing culture." Don't open with your degrees, awards, or office decor. Open with what you know and how it immediately helps the person watching.

The Hard Knockz Attention Framework

James Dumoulin grew The School of Hard Knockz from three 20-somethings with an idea to a $500K/month media machine with 1.9M YouTube subscribers. The strategic lessons for building an attention-based business apply directly to an LMFT building a content-driven practice.

📎 The School of Hard Knockz — James Dumoulin · Roe Magazine interview · YouTube 1.9M subscribers
"Where attention goes, revenue flows."

Hard Knockz built a $500K/month business not by having the best content — but by understanding that attention is the asset class. For an LMFT: every day you don't have a social presence, someone else's therapist content is building the trust bank your future client should have with you. Attention is a zero-sum game. Start claiming yours.

James Dumoulin — The School of Hard Knockz, Roe Magazine

1

The Pivot Principle — You Don't Have to Be the Only Expert

Hard Knockz's founding insight that changed everything

FOUNDATIONAL

The Hard Knockz origin story

Dumoulin started making business content as a 20-something and quickly realized: nobody wants business advice from a 20-year-old. So instead of positioning himself as the expert, he went to find the experts and made them accessible. He became the bridge between insight and audience. His value was curation, access, and the emotional arc — not the credentials.

Applied to your LMFT practice
You ARE the expert — unlike Hard Knockz, you have the clinical credential. But the lesson still applies: you don't have to be the only source of wisdom. Interview a peer therapist. Feature (anonymized) clinical observations. Quote research. Your voice as the contextualizer of clinical knowledge for real people is more powerful than lecturing alone.

Collaborate your way up the credibility ladder

"All you need is that one big feature," Dumoulin said. Once you land one larger account's collaboration, leverage it to get the next — credibility compounds. The first tag or collab from a bigger account is worth more than 100 posts alone.

Your collaboration ladder
Peer therapist with 2× your following → local wellness influencer → mental health advocate account → podcast guest appearance → expert collab post. Each one makes the next easier. Start with a collab Reel on a shared clinical topic with a peer who already has an audience.
2

Quantity Breeds Quality — Then Raise the Standard

The two-phase growth system Hard Knockz used to get from 0 to 1.9M subscribers

GROWTH SEQUENCE

Phase 1: Post more than feels comfortable

"Early on, they posted multiple times a day. Quantity is what breeds quality. You learn fast what the audience loves, and then you double down." Hard Knockz's first three years were trench days — posting constantly when almost nobody was watching. First $100K month didn't arrive until year 3.

For your practice — months 1–3
Post 5× per week without worrying about perfection. The first 50 videos are your research phase. You're learning which hooks land, which topics get saved, which formats get shared. You cannot know this from planning. You can only know it from posting. Don't optimize a car you haven't built yet.

Phase 2: Raise the standard, optimize for impact

"Now our standard keeps getting higher. I'd rather optimize for more views on a couple of videos than post a lot just to hit a quota." — Dumoulin. Once you know what works, fewer, better pieces of that specific thing outperforms volume for volume's sake.

For your practice — months 4+
Your analytics will show 3–5 topic/format combinations that consistently outperform. Scale those. Cut the rest. "Married couples having the same fight" content outperforming everything? Make 20 variations of that exact format — then make each one better than the last.

Outsource editing — it's a minimum-wage activity

"Get your editor to do it 80% as well as you can — then let it go. The founder's time is better spent on relationships, deals, and being on camera." — Dumoulin. Editing is a bottleneck that kills volume. Remove it.

What this looks like for an LMFT
Hire a Fiverr editor ($8–15/video) to add captions, trim pauses, and add background music. You show up, record 10 raw minutes, they deliver a polished reel. Your energy stays on recording more content — not cutting it. This single change can double your posting volume.
3

The Emotional Arc — The 30–60 Second Human Truth

Why Hard Knockz gets millions of views on the same simple question asked of everyone

CONTENT FORMAT

The Hard Knockz emotional arc formula

"Have you ever been broke? When? How did it feel? What did you do next?" The same question asked of billionaires, ex-cons, athletes, CEOs. The arc: vulnerability → the low point → the turn → the insight. Compressed to under 60 seconds. This emotional compression is why these clips hit millions of views.

Your LMFT version of this arc
"Have you ever been in a relationship where you loved someone but felt completely alone?" [pause — let it land] "Here's what that's actually about clinically..." "And here's what most couples never realize about it." Same arc, therapy niche. This structure reliably outperforms lecture-format content 3:1.

Making people feel safe — your unfair advantage

"He's calm, respectful, unthreatening. Even when someone says no, he asks again in a way that feels like an invitation." Dumoulin's greatest skill is the one therapists train for years: making people feel safe enough to share the real thing.

Your moat
You are clinically trained in the exact skill that makes Hard Knockz work on camera. The warmth, the non-judgment, the calm pace that invites honesty — you do this for a living. On camera, this reads as immediate, rare trust. Most creators have to work for years to develop what you already have. Use it consciously.

The democratization purpose

"How many people don't have access to this information? How many believe their dreams are unrealistic because everyone around them thinks small?" Hard Knockz makes billionaire insight accessible. You make clinical insight accessible to people who can't afford weekly therapy or don't know they need it yet.

Your version of this mission
Most people who need couples therapy don't know what it actually is, assume it costs $200/session out of pocket, or believe it's only for couples in crisis. Your content is their access point to knowledge that could change their relationship — before they ever pay for a session. This purpose is a content engine that never runs dry.
4

The Long Game — Consistency Before Payday

The timeline they don't show you on social media

MINDSET

The real Hard Knockz timeline

"Our first couple of years were trench days. We were just giving away value for free." Hard Knockz started in 2021. First $100K month didn't come until 2024. Now every month exceeds $500K. Those numbers came from three years of showing up when almost nobody was paying attention.

Your practice timeline expectation
Month 1–3: Small numbers. Post anyway.
Month 4–6: One video breaks out. Following jumps. Don't stop.
Month 7–12: Compound growth begins. Profile is a trust machine.
Year 2: Clients say "I found you on Instagram." Ads become supplemental, not primary.

Content is the main thing — everything else is downstream

"Content is the main thing. Double down, triple down, 10x that — and then build everything else." Dumoulin's hierarchy for Hard Knockz: content first. Ads, website, partnerships — all downstream from the attention content creates.

The hierarchy for your practice
Content → Profile visits → Trust → Headway bookings → Clients → Referrals → Group therapy → Scale. Everything flows from the content. Your Headway profile, your ads, your website — all of these are capture mechanisms for attention you've already built. Without the content, they're empty nets cast in empty water.

"The greatest skill set anybody can have in today's world is attention. Build it first. Build it obsessively. Everything else is downstream."

James Dumoulin — The School of Hard Knockz, Roe Magazine interview

Monthly Analytics Review Protocol

Every Monday — 15-minute review
1
Check saves + shares before views

Saves = they'll return to it. Shares = they sent it to their partner or friend. A post with 500 views and 80 saves is worth more than 5,000 views and 2 saves. These are your best signals.

2
Identify your top 3 topic-format combos

Not just topic — the combination matters. "Myth bust + 30-second direct to camera" may outperform "Education + carousel." Find the winning pairs, not just the winning subjects.

3
Check which videos drove profile visits

These are your acquisition videos. Make 10 variations of each. These are the videos that turn viewers into Headway page visitors — protect and scale them.

4
Flag top performers for repost next month

Magin ran his agency 2 months without making a fresh video — pure reposts of evergreen content. Clinical insight doesn't expire. Repost your top 3 every month without apology.

Decision framework
High saves + sharesScale this content type
High commentsControversy working — keep it
High profile visitsAcquisition content — scale immediately
High views, low saves/followsEntertainment not authority — adjust angle
Under 200 views, low engagementCut this format/topic combination
Magin's optimization rule: "Make more of the videos doing better — and make them better." Not more videos. More of the right video, made better. This is the entire system.

Where to Post on Craigslist

Posts get buried over time. Set a monthly calendar reminder to renew each post. Change the headline slightly each renewal — it reads as new to the algorithm and to repeat browsers.
